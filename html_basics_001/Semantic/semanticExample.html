What is Semantic HTML?

Semantic HTML uses meaningful tags like <header>, <nav>, <main>, and <article> to describe the purpose of the content they contain. This makes pages more accessible, better for SEO, and easier to maintain.

For instance, <main> is used for the core content, while <aside> holds related side content.

Why Use Semantics?

  • Accessibility: Screen readers understand your structure better.
  • SEO: Search engines rank you higher when content is meaningful.
  • Maintainability: Easier for devs to read and update.
  • Clarity: Your HTML tells a story—not just to browsers, but to people.
